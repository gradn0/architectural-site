My work

This house was designed on a very steeply sloping site with beautiful 180 degree sea views. The owner wanted a house in a Tuscan style but with a more contemporary feel. The result was a 4 bedroom home split over 3 levels. The lowest level accommodating a 4 car garage with workshop, laundry and staff quarters. The middle floor or pedestrian entry level consists of a spacious family lounge, reading area and large curved covered patio taking in the surrounding sea views. This level also includes the main bedroom, two other bedrooms and a family bathroom. The top floor allows for a large open plan lounge, dining and kitchen area with scullery and pantry. The lounge also opens up to the amazing views and includes a built in BBQ area.

This 4 bedroom home was designed and built on a large property in the countryside and expresses a relaxed formality.


The entrance being formal and symmetrical making use of Doric order columns on each side of a large front door that floats within a custom designed stained glass frame allowing ample light into the entrance hall. The transition from public to private space was important to the owner so the formal lounge and dining room were located each side of the the formal entrance space.

The more private spaces such as the family lounge, open plan kitchen / dining areas were located behind frosted glass doors providing screening from visitors.

Other features included a separate side entrance into a boot room and a direct access from the double garage into the kitchen via the laundry / scullery.

This project involved the design and delivery of the Building Control package following a successful planning application by others. The technical design provided the Contractor with an accurate set of drawings which included the construction plans, sections and details, Electrical layouts, drainage plans and also the coordination of the Structural design information to ensure that the home was built to the highest technical standards and complied with all the latest building regulations.

The owner of this terrace house wanted to revitalise his property and also open up the rear to communicate more directly with the garden. This transformation has been achieved by removing the old horizontal cladding and uPVC windows and replacing them with new through-colour render and black aluminium windows. This revitalisation to the property has been achieved whilst still maintaining harmony with the neighbouring properties. The interior was completely stripped out and transformed into a beautiful light-filled contemporary home.

This unique and inviting home has been designed to harmonise with the open countryside whilst still maintaining a contemporary feel.


The plan of the house has been kept very compact with all the service areas placed on the cooler side of the house whilst the living areas are located to maximise on sunlight and warmth. The dining room has been placed on the East side so that breakfast-time would be drenched in morning sun. The roof voids have been fully utilised as bonus spaces, making the cost to floor area ratio extremely economical whilst still creating some very interesting and quirky spaces .

The brief from this client was to design a new home on a relatively small site with lovely valley views to the south.


The style requested, was to be Tuscan but with a contemporary twist and the addition of a basement wine cellar. The house has been designed on three levels making use of the slope of the ground to minimize the amount of excavation required for the wine cellar. The living areas having been located to maximize on sunlight and warmth. The dining room has been placed on the west side with a covered patio shielding the living space from the harsh western sun whilst still communicating closely with the pool area. The kitchen, dining and family room are all open plan and open up onto the patio and private garden.
